note : "Stemma of all known versions of the Old French Tristan legend, from the lost archetypal poem, to the various translations. In addition to Bédier's reconstruction, shown here, Gertrude Schoepperle (Tristan and Isolt : a study of the sources of the romance, Frankfurt, 1913) postulates an Old French model older than the archetype, the Estoire, that she calls B, and is somewhat equivalent to Wendelin Foerster's Ur-Urtristan. Contrarily to Bédier, she does not take the existence of a limited duration of the love potion in Béroul and Eilhart as an argument for a common lost model, but makes it a feature of the original, modified by Thomas, which seems quite convincing. In that case, y could be removed. See also the account by J. Douglas Bruce in Modern Language note, 29-7 (1914), p. 213, https://www.jstor.org/stable/pdf/2916175.pdf. Other complements by Hofer, Stefan, « Streitfragen zur altfranzösischen Literatur », Zeitschrift für romanische Philologie, 65:4-6, 1949, p. 257-288. DOI: 10.1515/zrph.1949.65.4-6.257, and Varvaro, Alberto, « La teoria dell'archetipo tristaniano », Romania, 88, 1967, p. 13-58. DOI: 10.3406/roma.1967.2595."
